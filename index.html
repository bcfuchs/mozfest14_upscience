Split Atoms!

Build your own particle detector.

Particle Clicker

A roll-your-own from the authors of Particle Clicker

First, we will give a short overview of how particle detectors work and explain why they're useful to study physics.

Next we will guide you through creating your own detector visualization with Javascript and HTML5.

Starting from a blank HTML canvas, we will introduce detector components and physics concepts one by one, until your detector is complete.

Build a Collider!

Run a Virtual Particle Collider from your browser

CernVM Web API

We'll show you how to use the CernVM WebAPI to build a fancy interface for controlling our Virtual Particle Collider.

We'll help you set up a local development environment and guide you on hooking up the Web API with Javascript and HTML5.

We'll also explain about the physics that is taking place.

And if we have enough time left, we might even connect the event information to the interfaces built by the Particle Clickers team

Go Big!

Learn how to crowdsource your science

CrowdCrafting: Empowering citizens to solve non computable problems.

Take part in real science projects hosted on the citizen science crowdsourcing platform crowdcrafting.org and learn about PyBossa, the open source technology behind it, that lets you create your own 'upscience' projects.

First, we'll give you a quick tour of the coolest projects in crowdcrafting.org while we explain the idea behind PyBossa. You'll also get a chance to contribute to those projects and gather some ideas for what it is to come later.

Next we'll take you through a step-by-step tutorial for creating your own project in Pybossa.

Go Small!

Nanotech tools for schools and citizens.

LEGO2NANO

Learn about LEGO2NANO. We'll tell you about the progress we've been making and the challenges ahead.

You'll learn about our project to develop a low cost atomic force microscope for use in schools in China, which brought together nanotechnologists, educationalists, artists and designers.

Help us brainstorm ideas for our latest project—an online platform for schools to share data and perform collaborative research.

About us

Upscience

Hey, citizen scientists!

You don't have to be a professor to make and launch real science projects on the Web.

New Web-based tools make it a cinch to start high-impact citizen science projects without the strictures imposed by traditional academic research.

It's called Upscience.

At our workshop, you'll learn about upscience projects like browser-based particle physics games made by students from CERN, community-driven crowdsourcing projects to classify images of fracking in the US or collect images of drought in Africa, simple browser-based approaches to build distributed volunteer supercomputers, and how to roll your own low-cost science tools.

We'll also discuss ways for people who are not online at all to participate in upscience.
